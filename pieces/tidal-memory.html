Tidal Memory

Abstract Art February 2026

Philosophy

Tidal Memory is the record of forces written in water. Every shoreline is a palimpsest — layers of tidal action inscribed and overwritten, each wave depositing its signature and erasing the last. This piece renders that accumulated record as pure visual form: horizontal undulations that carry the memory of every tide that ever turned.

Visual Language

The visual language is the wave — not a single wave, but dozens of overlapping wave systems at different frequencies, amplitudes, and phases. Long-period swells roll through with the patience of ocean basins. Short-period ripples dance on their surfaces. Where waves constructively interfere, peaks rise with doubled intensity. Where they destructively interfere, brief moments of calm open like windows in a storm.

Color

Deep blue-blacks form the ground — the color of water under a moonless sky. Phosphor green traces the crests of the largest waves, as if bioluminescent organisms ride the surface tension. Gold appears in the interference peaks. Rose marks the erosion zones: places where overlapping wave action has worn away at the fabric of the composition itself.

Scale & Composition

The lower region of the canvas shows the memory: sediment layers left by receding tides. Horizontal strata of compressed wave action, each layer a slightly different hue and density, recording thousands of cycles compressed into geological time. The waves above are the present; the strata below are the past.

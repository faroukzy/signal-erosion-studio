Spectral Decay

Abstract Art February 2026

Philosophy

Spectral Decay is the study of signal degradation as an aesthetic event. Every transmitted signal — radio wave, digital packet, spoken word — begins its journey in a state of pristine clarity and ends, inevitably, in noise. This piece captures that full arc: the crisp, confident waveform at the moment of transmission, the gradual accumulation of interference, and the final dissolution into beautiful static.

Visual Language

The composition reads from top to bottom like a page of music: the opening bars are clean, measured, predictable. Horizontal waveforms trace across the canvas with mathematical precision — pure sine curves, sharp square waves, regular pulse trains. As the eye moves downward, entropy enters. Frequencies begin to jitter. Amplitudes wander. Ghost echoes appear — faint duplicates displaced in time and phase. In the final third, structure surrenders. Waveforms scatter into particle fields.

Color

The cleanest signals glow in phosphor green and gold — the colors of instrument readouts and oscilloscope traces. As degradation progresses, rose and amber intrude — the warm tones of analog distortion. In the final noise field, all colors are present in faint, equal measure — a visual white noise that mirrors the acoustic.

Scale & Composition

The transition from order to entropy operates across the full vertical extent of the canvas. Macro waveforms establish rhythm at the top; micro-particle scatter creates texture at the bottom. The eye traces the full arc of degradation, discovering that noise is not the absence of signal but its most democratic state.

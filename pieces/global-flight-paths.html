Global Flight Paths

Data Visualization February 2026

Philosophy

Global Flight Paths maps the invisible highways of modern aviation — the great circle routes that connect the world's airports in a web of human mobility. Every day, over 100,000 commercial flights trace arcs through the atmosphere.

Their accumulated paths form a network of extraordinary density and beauty. This piece renders that network as luminous cartography: each route a glowing arc, each hub a radiant node, the entire system a portrait of how humanity has compressed the planet.

Visual Language

The composition uses an equirectangular projection — continents suggested by the faintest possible outlines, just enough to provide geographic context. Each flight path follows a great circle arc, projected onto the flat canvas as graceful curves.

Route brightness and thickness encode frequency: the London-New York corridor blazes as a thick golden band. Major hubs — London, Dubai, Singapore, JFK, Beijing, Frankfurt — bloom as bright nodes where dozens of routes converge.

Color

Golden and amber tones define the flight paths, with route intensity modulating brightness. Hub nodes glow with concentrated luminosity. The faintest continental outlines in muted tones provide geographic orientation without competing with the flight data.

Data

The overall pattern reveals the geography of power: dense networks over the North Atlantic and East Asia, sparse connections over central Africa and the Pacific, chokepoint corridors through the Middle East. A latitude-longitude grid in the faintest tone provides orientation.

Ocean Thermal Gradient

Data Visualization February 2026

Philosophy

Ocean Thermal Gradient is a vertical portrait of the ocean's hidden architecture — the invisible layers of temperature that stratify the world's waters from sun-warmed surface to lightless abyss. The ocean is not a uniform body; it is a layer cake of thermal zones.

Each zone has its own physics, its own ecology, its own relationship to the planet's climate system. This piece renders that hidden architecture visible through the language of color and gradient.

Visual Language

The composition is a cross-section: full width, read from top to bottom. The surface zone blazes with warm reds and ambers — 25°C of solar heat stored in the upper hundred meters. Below it, the thermocline drops like a curtain — the sharpest thermal boundary on Earth.

Below the thermocline, the deep ocean extends to the abyss in an endless gradient of cold blues and teals. At 4,000 meters, the water holds steady near 2°C. At the very bottom, the hadal zone: pressures that crush, temperatures near freezing, and a darkness that has never known light.

Color

Warm reds and ambers at the surface transition through the thermocline to deep blues and teals in the abyss. Faint horizontal current lines trace the movement of water masses — warm currents flowing poleward near the surface, cold deep currents returning toward the equator.

Data

Depth markers on the left edge provide scale in monospace type: 0m, 200m, 1000m, 4000m, 11000m. Temperature annotations on the right edge translate color into Celsius. Current flow lines trace thermohaline circulation. The overall effect is a geological survey rendered in the language of heat.

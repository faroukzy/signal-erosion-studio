Circadian Rhythm Cycle

Infographic February 2026

Philosophy

Circadian Rhythm Cycle maps the human body's 24-hour biological clock as a circular infographic — a temporal diagram where time flows clockwise around the circumference and the body's internal systems pulse, rise, and fall in orchestrated waves. Melatonin surges in darkness. Cortisol spikes at dawn. Core body temperature peaks in late afternoon.

Each biological event is timed with precision that predates alarm clocks by millions of years. This piece makes that internal choreography visible — the body as a precision timekeeper, its rhythms revealed in concentric rings of data.

Visual Language

The composition is radial. The 24-hour clock face occupies the center, with midnight at the top and noon at the bottom. Four concentric rings radiating outward encode different biological systems: melatonin, cortisol, body temperature, and alertness. Each ring undulates according to real circadian data — rising and falling in smooth curves that reflect the body's internal tide.

Key events are annotated around the circumference: deepest sleep at 2 AM, sharpest blood pressure rise at 6:45 AM, fastest reaction time at 3:30 PM, highest body temperature at 7 PM. These readings appear like instruments from a biological panel.

Color

Each biological system has its own hue: melatonin in deep rose (the hormone of darkness), cortisol in amber (the hormone of waking), body temperature in gold (thermal energy), alertness in teal (mental clarity). Where systems overlap — cortisol suppressing melatonin at dawn — the colors interact at their boundaries. Brightness intensifies with biological activity.
